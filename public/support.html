Frequently Asked Questions

How do I join a guild?
You can join a guild in two ways:
  • Via invite code: Ask a guild member for their invite code, then go to the Guild Hub and click "Join Guild" to enter the code.
  • Via invite link: Click on a guild invite link shared by a member, and you'll be taken directly to the join page.
Once you join, you'll see your guild's activity feed and leaderboard. You can be a member of multiple guilds!
What are streak shields and how do they work?
Streak shields protect your reading streak when you miss a day. You earn one shield for every 7 consecutive days of reading. If you miss a day and have shields available, one shield is automatically consumed to maintain your streak. Without shields, missing a day resets your streak to 0. Shields are your safety net for busy days!
How do reading plans work?
Biblical Battle Plans offers 4 plan types:
  • Sequential: Read straight through the Bible from Genesis to Revelation
  • Horner's System: Rotate through 10 different reading lists (Gospels, Epistles, OT, etc.) daily
  • 52-Week Plan: Balanced reading of OT, NT, and Psalms over one year
  • Free Reading: Log any chapters at your own pace with no set schedule
Start a plan from the Plans page, and it becomes your active campaign. Track your progress on the Dashboard!
How do I delete my account?
To delete your account:
  1. Go to your Profile page (tap your avatar in the top right)
  2. Scroll down to the "Danger Zone" section
  3. Click "Delete Account"
  4. Confirm the deletion
Warning: This action is permanent and irreversible. All your reading progress, streaks, achievements, and guild memberships will be permanently deleted.
How do I change my notification times? (iOS)
On iOS devices, you can set daily reading reminders:
  1. Go to your Profile page
  2. Find the "Notifications" section
  3. Enable notifications and set your preferred reminder time
  4. Save your changes
The app will automatically cancel notifications for days when you've completed your reading. You can disable notifications at any time from the same settings.
What are the different plan types and which should I choose?
  • Sequential Plan: Best for first-time Bible readers or those wanting chronological order. Read Genesis to Revelation straight through.
  • Horner's System: For experienced readers who want variety. Read from 10 different bookmarks daily, cycling through the entire Bible.
  • 52-Week Plan: Balanced approach mixing OT, NT, Psalms, and Proverbs. Complete the Bible in one year with diverse daily readings.
  • Free Reading: Maximum flexibility. Log any chapters without a structured plan. Great for topical studies or casual reading.
Choose based on your reading style and goals. You can switch plans anytime!
How are ranks and achievements calculated?
Your rank is based on your current reading streak:
  • Recruit: 0-6 days
  • Squire: 7-29 days
  • Knight: 30-89 days
  • Champion: 90-179 days
  • Legend: 180-364 days
  • Immortal: 365+ days
Achievements unlock at milestones like completing books, reaching streak thresholds, and joining guilds. Check your Profile to see all available achievements!
Can I use the app offline?
Yes! Biblical Battle Plans supports offline functionality. Your reading progress is cached locally and will sync with the server when you reconnect to the internet. However, features that require real-time data (like guild leaderboards and activity feeds) require an active connection.
Can I be in multiple guilds?
Yes! You can join as many guilds as you want. This is great for different accountability groups (church small group, family, friends, etc.). Each guild has its own leaderboard and activity feed, and your reading progress appears in all your guilds.

Reporting Bugs

If you encounter a bug or technical issue, please include the following information in your report:

  • Device type (iPhone, iPad, desktop browser, etc.)
  • Operating system version (iOS 17, macOS Sonoma, etc.)
  • Browser version (if using web app)
  • Steps to reproduce the issue
  • Screenshots or screen recordings (if possible)
  • What you expected to happen vs. what actually happened
